Design reasoning: The AI search bar is the primary CTA — placed centrally above the fold so it is the first thing a user sees. Design reasoning: The location picker is anchored in the navbar center — persistent and always editable, auto-filled when signed in to reduce friction. Design reasoning: A cascading reveal (period → time → seats) prevents cognitive overload — the seat map only appears after a time is committed. Concert layout places VIP seats at the front (closest to stage), standard at the back, mirroring real venue logic. Booked seats show ✕ and are greyed out rather than hidden, preserving the user's spatial understanding of the venue. The "Proceed to Book" button is disabled until at least one seat is selected, preventing empty-state errors downstream.

Design reasoning: A vertical timeline replaces the conventional summary card — each dot represents one decision the user made, in the order they made it. This mirrors the journey already taken, making review fast and intuitive. Attendee details are auto-filled from the account to reduce friction. The price breakdown is a transparent line-item receipt — surfacing the convenience fee here (not at payment) avoids a dark pattern. "Edit seats" goes back without resetting event or timing, preventing frustration on return.
